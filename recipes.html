The history of cookies dates back to 7th century Persia, one of the first regions to cultivate sugar. These early cookies were simple and small, made as test cakes to check oven temperatures. The idea quickly spread to Europe, with each country developing its unique variations. By the 14th century, cookies had become a popular treat in royal courts and street markets across Europe. When European settlers arrived in America, they brought their favorite cookie recipes with them, leading to the rich and diverse cookie culture we enjoy today.

The history of cake dates back to ancient times, with the earliest versions resembling more bread-like creations sweetened with honey. Ancient Egyptians are credited with making the first cakes, which were often used in religious ceremonies. The Greeks and Romans further refined cake-making by adding nuts and dried fruits. During the Renaissance, advances in baking techniques and the introduction of refined sugar from the New World led to lighter, more delicate cakes. By the 17th century, cake-making had become an art form in Europe, with the development of elaborate, multi-layered confections. Today, cake is a universal symbol of celebration, enjoyed in countless varieties around the world.
